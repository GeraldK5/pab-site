SUB REGIONAL STRATEGIC PLANS

In 2021, the President of the Republic of Uganda, Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Tibuhaburwa Museveni abolished the Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation (MoSTI) and created a new entity under the Office of the President called the Science, Technology and Innovation – Office of President (STI-OP). This entity was created by a directive with which were a number of Bureaus of which one is called Productivity Acceleration Bureau. The mandate of the Productivity Acceleration Bureau is “Coordinating and facilitating research, development, technology transfer, and commercialization of innovations   aimed at increasing productivity in key areas of the national economy”.

To fulfil this mandate, the Productivity Acceleration Bureau was directed to execute nine (9) key functions of which one is to “Initiate the development of plans, strategies and programs for Productivity Acceleration in the Country”. In implementing this key function, the Productivity Acceleration Bureau conducted a sub regional wealth analysis using 2022 UBOS data. This analysis indicated that Karamoja was the last in wealth, followed by Bukedi then Busoga. Due to the dynamics of Karamoja, a decision was made to start with Bukedi sub region in 2024 developing a plan/strategy for accelerating wealth creation which after Busoga would follow.

On the 22nd Aug 2024, the President of the Republic of Uganda, Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Tibuhaburwa Museveni, while gracing the Thanks giving of the Hon. Minister for Science, Technology and Innovation, Dr. Monica Musenero Masanza, launched the Bukedi Sub Region Strategic Plan for Accelerated Wealth Creation. A few months later, in November 2024, during the Presidential PDM tour in Bukedi, while at Butaleja District Head Quarters meeting Bukedi Leader, H.E. Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Tibuhaburwa Museveni, directed that all sub regions be assisted develop strategic plans for wealth creation and that Government would support sub regions with UGX 50 Billion annually to implement the plan. The plan has been implemented for now a year and a great change has been registered with 38% of the households aware of the strategic plan and over 50% of the farms in the prioritized enterprises were established after the Launch.

As Productivity Acceleration Bureau of STI-OP was preparing to start on Busoga, Hon. Minister for Presidency, Milly Babalanda Babirye who had heard about the success approached Hon. Minister, Dr. Monica Musenero Masanza asking her to come to Busoga and do what STI-OP did in Bukedi, She emphasized that her people of Buyende District and Busoga at large need to overcome poverty using the same tool, the strategic plan for accelerated wealth Creation. Dr. Monica Musenero Masanza responded immediately by sending the Productivity Acceleration Bureau Team to start on Busoga.

THE PROCESS OF DEVELOPING THE SUB REGIONAL STRATEGIC PLANS

The processing of developing a sub regional strategic plan is as follows;

  1. Short status survey: This involves collection of data on the common enterprises and success stories. This was done in Buyende District.  
  2. Think Tanking/Draft one development: This step involves selection of passionate individuals and facilitating them to think and develop draft one. This was completed for Busoga on the 8th January 2026.
  3. Sample Multilevel Consultative meetings: This includes engagement of several stakeholders including Parish leaders, PDM leaders, sub county leaders, district leaders, champion leaders through kibalo to prioritize enterprises.
  4. Development of the fair copy of the final Strategic Plan: This includes synthesis of consultation inputs and development the fair copy.
  5. Presentation to The President of the Republic of Uganda: This includes a meeting with H.E presenting the fair copy and seek guidance on the priorities of the Plan.
  6. Development of ready to launch version: This includes incorporation of inputs by H.E.
  7. Launch of the Strategic Plan: This activity is graced by the President of the Republic of Uganda and official launch and publication of the strategic plan

BUSOGA SUB REGION STRATEGY PLAN FOR ACCELERATED WEALTH CREATION

The Busoga Sub Regional Strategic Plan for Accelerated Wealth Creation, draft one has been completed and ready for public consultations and inputs.

WHAT MDAS ARE INVOLVED?

 SThe Science, Technology and Innovation – Office of the President (STI-OP) in Partnership with State House and National Planning Authority are facilitating the development of the Busoga Sub Region Strategy Plan for Accelerated Wealth Creation. From the 15th – 19th December 2025 and 5th – 8th January 2026, the three MDAs led by the STI-OP facilitated the think tanking session that developed the first draft of the sub regional plan.

WHO IS THE TEAM DRAFTING THE PLAN?

A team of Busoga community individuals were identified and invited to freely/probono invest in time and brain to become the Busoga Think Tankers. This team of 17 with at least one representative from each District including the city constituted the adhoc think tank team for Busoga and drafted the first draft of the strategic plan.

WHAT WAS THE METHODOLOGY USED?

Since the 1990s, the NRM government, championed by H.E. Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Tibuhaburwa Museveni has been preaching the gospel of “Kibalo” which means profitability analysis for every enterprise one engages in. This has been going until in 2022 when H.E. Gen. Yoweri Kaguta Tibuhaburwa Museveni directed that Uganda must qualitatively leap economically from the GDP USD 50 Billion to USD 500 Billion by 2040. H. E explained that once science, technology and innovation is applied in every thing Ugandans do, it will transition the country into the Knowledge economy. This was to be supported by mass creation of value with “Kibalo”. In short, whether you are farming or processing or offering service, you had to apply “Kibalo” to decide which enterprise to start.

STI-OP led by the Hon. Minister Dr. Monica Musenero Masanza took it and developed a tool, The “Kibalo” Tool for enterprise profitability analysis as the President has for long been teaching the country to do. The tool was used to prioritize the enterprises Bukedi sub region should focus on and develop a strategic plan. The same were used for Busoga Sub region where the think tankers used the Kibalo tool to assess viability of over 30 commodity value chains. To determine whether a commodity value chain is worth choosing, the teams calculate the limiting node of the value chain which is the primary raw material production “e.g The Farm”. The profit/loss made per year or season are estimated and converted into profit/loss per million invested and ranked from largest to smallest. To ensure the chosen commodity value chain is reliable, the tool further factors in Market stability and re-ranks according profit per million invested by largest market size.
